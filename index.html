Johan Cruyff

"Soccer is simple, but it is difficult to play simple."

  • 1947 - Born on 25th April
  • 1957 - Joins Ajax youth team
  • 1964 - Makes first team debut on 15th November. Scores goal in a 3-1 defeat by GVAV (who later became FC Groningen)
  • 1966 - Scores 25 goals in 23 games as Ajax win the Dutch Eredivisie
  • 1968 - Wins league for third successive season
  • 1969 - Ajax runner-up to AC Milan in the European Cup Final
  • 1971 - Made Dutch and European Footballer of the Year for 1971 as Ajax win the first of three successive European Cups
  • 1973 - Joins Barcelona for a world record 6 million guilders (approx 2 million dollars)
  • 1974 - Wins La Liga as Barcelona defeat rivals Real Madrid 5-0 at the Santiago Bernabeu in Madrid. Runner-up in 1974 World Cup final, where Holland lose to the hosts West Germany in Munich
  • 1980 - Rejoins Ajax after several years playing in the North American Soccer League. Two further Eredivisie titles follow, before a controversial move to rivals Feyenoord Rotterdam
  • 1984 - Wins league at Feyenoord, retires on 13th May 1984
  • 1986 - Appointed Ajax manager
  • 1988 - Appointed Barcelona manager. Four succesive La Liga titles follow between 1990 and 1994
  • 1992 - Guides Barcelona to their first European Cup triumph at Wembley

"As a player, he turned football into an art form. Johan came along and revolutionised everything. The modern-day Barça started with him, he is the expression of our identity, he brought us a style of football we love."

—Barcelona's ex-president Joan Laporta, 2010
